Main Information

Species Green Anaconda
Scientific Name Eunectes murinus
Length Up to 30 feet (9 meters)
Weight Up to 250 kg

Description

The anaconda is one of the largest and most powerful snakes in the world. Known for its incredible size and aquatic skills, it can be found in the swamps and rivers of South America. Despite its intimidating appearance, it typically avoids humans.

Origin

Native to the Amazon and Orinoco river basins, the anaconda has long fascinated scientists and adventurers. Ancient myths often portrayed it as a mythical creature of great power, feared and respected by local communities.

Habitat

Anacondas live in marshes, swamps, and slow-moving streams, primarily in the Amazon and Orinoco rainforests. Their aquatic habitat allows them to move silently and hunt effectively.

Diet

These constrictors feed on fish, birds, mammals, and occasionally even caimans. They kill by wrapping around their prey and suffocating it.
